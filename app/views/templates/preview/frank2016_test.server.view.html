You're invited to join us for frank, the foremost gathering of movement builders and rabble-rousers who use strategic communications to drive change. Each year, strategists, scholars, journalists, storytellers, non-profit leaders and activists come to the historic Hippodome Theatre in Gainesville, Florida, to share the best of what they know about using strategic communications to drive change.

Last year's speakers included Anastasia Khoo of the Human Rights Campaign, Grist's Chip Giller, cognitive scientist Stephen Pinker and NPR's Shankar Vedantam. Check out videos of last year's speakers and a highlight reel here.

When you come to frank, you join a vibrant community of the world's most sophisticated communicators — people taking on big challenges like climate change, marriage equality, poverty and better health.

This is your personal invitation to frank. It's kind of a big deal. We put a lot of thought into who we invite to the gathering to make sure we have a balance of perspectives from the corporate, foundation, academic and non-profit sectors.

At frank, you'll get great talks rooted in fresh scientific insights from scholars like Brendan Nyhan, lessons learned in the front lines of change from activists like Ai-Jen Poo mixed up with plenty of opportunities to spend time with speakers and other franksters in Gainesville's vibrant downtown scene.

We'll kick things off with a Welcome Rally Tuesday, February 23 at 6 pm, and the program will begin in earnest at noon the following day. We'll wrap up Friday, February 26, at 1:30 pm.
